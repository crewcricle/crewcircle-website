## Monday: The Problem

Most Australian small businesses spend 10 to 20 hours a week on admin that doesn't generate revenue.

Replying to Google reviews. Sorting receipts for BAS. Updating your business profile. Answering the same customer questions over and over.

I spoke with a café owner last month who spends two hours every Sunday night writing review responses. Not because she wants to, but because she knows businesses with more Google replies rank higher locally. Two hours she could spend with her family, or on growing the business, or on literally anything else.

The interesting thing is that most of these tasks follow patterns. They're repetitive. They follow rules. And that's exactly the kind of work AI is good at today. Not replacing your judgment. Just handling the drudgery so you can focus on the work that actually matters.

This week I'm sharing what practical AI looks like for Aussie small businesses. No enterprise sales decks. No "digital transformation" frameworks. Just specific tools that solve specific problems.

## Wednesday: The Local Business Angle

If you run a local business in Australia, your online presence probably needs more attention than you can give it.

LocalMate was built for exactly that. It handles five jobs in one dashboard:

1. Review Guard drafts replies to your Google and Yelp reviews. You approve or tweak, and it posts.
2. Rank Report tracks your local search ranking every week.
3. Competitor Watch monitors what nearby competitors are doing online.
4. Rebook follows up with lapsed customers via SMS and email.
5. Menu Sync pushes updates from a Google Sheet to Google Business Profile and Square.

For most local businesses, that's several hours back every week. Not because of anything fancy, but because these are repetitive tasks that follow patterns. AI handles them well.

The thing I'm most proud of with LocalMate is that you don't need a technical background to use it. Connect your Google Business Profile, switch on the tools you need, and let it run.

## Thursday: What to Try First

I get asked a lot: "Where should I actually start with AI?"

My answer is always the same. Don't try everything. Pick the one thing that costs you the most time right now, and solve that first.

If you're an accountant, try asking an AI research tool a tax question about a recent ATO ruling. You'll know within five minutes if it saves you time.

If you own a local business, connect your Google Business Profile and start automating your review replies. That's the quickest win.

If you're a sole trader who wants something simple, try a free Chrome extension that lets you look up ABN details and check ATO rates without leaving your browser.

The point is to start small. If it works, add more. If it doesn't, you haven't lost anything.

You don't need a digital strategy. You need a Tuesday afternoon and a problem that annoys you.
