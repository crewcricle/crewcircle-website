## SEGMENT 1: The admin problem (0:45 to 2:00)

**[Screen recording or simple graphics showing the tasks.]**

Here's the reality for most small businesses in Australia. You're spending somewhere between 10 and 20 hours a week on tasks that don't actually make you money.

Replying to Google reviews. Sorting receipts for your bookkeeper. Updating your Google Business Profile. Checking ATO deadlines. Following up with customers who haven't come back in a while.

None of that is the work you got into business to do. But all of it affects your revenue. If you don't reply to reviews, your Google ranking drops. If you don't follow up with lapsed customers, they go somewhere else. If you miss a BAS deadline, you're paying penalties.

The thing is, most of these tasks follow patterns. They're repetitive. And that's exactly where AI is useful today. Not for writing your business plan. Not for replacing your expertise. For handling the stuff that takes time but doesn't require your brain.

## SEGMENT 2: What this looks like for accounting firms (2:00 to 3:30)

**[Screen recording of TaxFlowAI in action.]**

Let me show you the accounting angle first.

If you run a tax practice, you spend a lot of time on ATO research. Looking up rulings. Cross-referencing determinations. Drafting responses to ATO letters. It's slow, it's fiddly, and even experienced professionals spend hours on it every week.

We built an tool called TaxFlowAI for exactly this. It's a research assistant built specifically for Australian tax professionals.

You type in a question, something like "What are the current rules for work-related car expense deductions?" and it searches through actual ATO public rulings, tax determinations, and legislation. It gives you an answer, and it shows you every source it used. So you can check it yourself. You can verify it against what you know.

You can also upload an ATO letter, and it'll draft a response for you. You're not sending that response blind, you're reviewing it, adding your professional judgment, and then sending it. But the heavy lifting of the first draft is done.

The key thing is that it understands the Australian tax system specifically. This isn't a generic AI that's been trained on US tax law. It's built for the ATO. For Australian rulings. For how our system actually works.

If you want to try it, it's at taxflow.crewcircle.com.au. You can try it free.

## SEGMENT 3: What this looks like for local businesses (3:30 to 5:15)

**[Screen recording of LocalMate dashboard.]**

Now let me show you the local business angle.

Say you run a restaurant, a salon, a mechanic, a dental practice, anything with a physical location. Your Google Business Profile is probably one of your most important marketing channels. But keeping it active takes time.

We built LocalMate to handle that. It does five things.

First, Review Guard. It reads your new Google and Yelp reviews and drafts replies for you. You approve them, tweak them, or write your own. But you're not staring at a blank text box at 9pm on a Sunday.

Second, Rank Report. Every week it checks where you rank in local search results and tells you whether you're going up or down. No more guessing.

Third, Competitor Watch. It keeps an eye on what your nearby competitors are doing online so you're not operating in the dark.

Fourth, Rebook. This one follows up with customers who haven't visited in a while, via SMS or email. Getting a lapsed customer back costs a fraction of finding a new one.

Fifth, Menu Sync. If you run a restaurant or cafe, you change a price in a Google Sheet, and it updates Google Business Profile and Square automatically. Change it once, it updates everywhere.

That's five jobs you're either doing manually, paying someone to do, or quietly ignoring because you're too busy. For most local businesses, this saves several hours a week.

You can check it out at localmate.crewcircle.com.au.
